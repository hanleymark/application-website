MARK HANLEY

VISION IMPAIRED DEVELOPER

Introduction

My name is Mark Hanley, and I'm going to tell you why I strongly believe that I possess the skills, experience and motivation to become a key member of the next team of F&C future developers!

My story (abridged)

I've always loved coding;

From silly animations and games back in the distant days of the home-micro revolution when I was a kid, to coding up Towers of Hanoi solutions in Excel to See If It Can Be Done.

I've always harboured the ambition to make coding my full-time job. However, having been a teacher for over twenty years, I've had to fit in the fun little programming projects around my main job. Consequently, my tinkering with Python, Flash/Actionscript, C#, Excel macros and more... has never attained the depth and saturation of knowledge required to make the leap into serious software development.

Then something happened...

On Friday 13th March 2020, while I was just about to enjoy my morning coffee before heading off to work, the entire right half of my visual field in both eyes went black. Disappeared. In a matter of seconds, without warning or pain, I permanently lost half my vision.

What I didn't know was that, while I had been sleepily preparing my coffee, a tiny blood clot had formed in my body and made its way up to the left occipital lobe in my brain and wreaked havoc in my visual cortex.

Cut to now, 2022. Thanks to meds and a minor operation to patch a hole in my heart, what happened to me on that day is extremely unlikely to happen again. It has taken time and a lot of learning, but I've come to terms with my disability.

I may have lost my vision, but I opened my eyes. Life is short and things can change big-style in the blink of an eye. Make time to chase your dreams.

My dream is to be a web developer. I also believe passionately that the web must be accessible to all. I want to use my understanding of vision loss and its challenges, to build web sites that are truly accessible to vision-impaired people.
